This past summer (2021), I interned at Facebook as a Front-end Engineering Intern. Over the course of my internship I worked on a new internal tooling experience, bringing all internal tools under one roof with a uniform UI and significantly easier onboarding experience. While migrating each internal tool to the new experience, I was able to interact with and collaborate with many teams, engineers, and designers. Additionally, I collaborated closely with another intern on areas in which our projects overlapped. This reduced extraneous work and allowed us to focus on building better React components that could be used by us both and others. Towards the end of the Summer, as I completed my project, I demoed my work to key users in order to incorporate customer feedback into the product. In addition to the work I did on my project, I added logging to most components / interactions on the new experience, and I created a dashboard to view countless stats about the product's usage; this allows the team to make educated decision on what to focus their time on.

Overall, this internship was a great experience. I gained many new technical skills, including industry level React and GraphQl, and I learned a lot about working on a team and collaborating closely with many engineers & product designers.

Last summer (2020), I returned to The Cooper Union Summer STEM program as a teaching assistant for the Robotics Crash Course. Due to COVID, the usual six-week course was divided into two three-week sessions of roughly twenty high-schoolers to allow more students to participate. Throughout the course, I, along with three others, directed the students in assembling a car containing an arduino and various sensors and taught the students the necessary software skills in order to program the robot to avoid obstacles. I created presentations and taught classes on Git, GitHub, and various topics in C++. While teaching over video-chat had its challenges, it was a rewarding experience; I, along with my coworkers, were able to cause students who began the course with no engineering experience to complete the course interested in entering the field. Teaching this course allowed me to review the fundamentals of arduino and C++, while researching new topics in order to answer difficult questions asked by students.
